About Sally Face

Sally Face is a dark, psychological horror point-and-click- advernture game that was created by Steve Gabry, or by his developer name Portable Moose. This game was all made with just a one-man army, so Steve contributed by creating the illustrations, writing, music, coding, and many more to make it all come to fruition.

Puzzle-solving, exploring, and interacting with the game's environment and characters is crucial to understand the game's narrative. The game consists of five episodes. The first episode was released on August 16, 2016 on itch.io, then on Steam at December 15, 2016.

The main character is Sal Fisher (Sally Face himself!) and his rag tag group of ghost buster friends, Larry Johnson (his best friend), Ashley Campbell, and Todd Morrison.

Sal had moved in to Addison Apartments with his single father from New Jersey for a fresh start after his mother's death many years ago. This move changes Sal's life as he encounters the ghostly residents that still live in the Apartments and a dark cult lurking within that might have their hands on the entire town of Nockfell. It's up to Sal and his friends to uncover the truth from within and stop the cult's takeover.

At the final episode, after Sal's execution, Ashley being the last sane and living being of her friend group left tries her best in bringing Sal back to life through her arm. The pair storm in to the cult's main hide out after planting an explosive to try and save Todd who was captured and infected by the Red Eyes (the new physical host for the cult". They encounter Larry on the way and together they defeat the cult, but we're told that there will be a second game coming soon so it might not be the end for them yet!

Why I Chose This Game

Sally Face is an indie game that has been near and dear to my heart back when I was in highschool when I was emotionally at my lowest point. This game was recommended to me by one of my friends at the time and it opened a whole new world for me. I was finally able to get out of my art block and started drawing and creating again because of this game. The story and its characters drew me in, and I just wanted to expand upon it with my own touch. I still love this game to this very day and might purchase the game myself other than just watching YouTubers once the second game is live.

Sally Face vs. Jacques Lacan's Mirror Stage Theory

Sal has a literal, physical barrier between how he is externally perceived in the world and his inner self. His real and true self that is hiding all of his trauma and backstory is locked within and we the players discover his past bit by bit each episode, despite him being our main character and who we control the most. Throughout Sal's interactions with other characters, some assume his gender or whether he could possibly be in a gang due to his mask. His prosthetic that he received due to an early childhood accident shaped and curated a fundamental literal and symbolic barrier between his real, not so-real self.
